1.5 Optimization Theory

Optimization theory explains why training can work even when the loss landscape is non-convex, high-dimensional, noisy, and impossible to solve exactly.

Почему важно

Любое обучение модели — это оптимизация. Convex vs non-convex, seddle points, local minima — не теория ради теории, а то, почему Adam работает лучше SGD в определённых задачах.

TL;DR

Classical optimization asks for global minima. Deep learning often works with non-convex landscapes, stochastic gradients, overparameterization, and approximate solutions. The key research questions are: why does optimization find useful solutions, and why do some solutions generalize better than others?

Why this matters

If you only know gradient descent as "move downhill", deep learning looks mysterious. Optimization theory gives language for saddle points, curvature, flatness, sharpness, stochasticity, implicit regularization, and why bigger models can sometimes generalize better.

Use this topic to understand
  • Why non-convex neural networks can still train.
  • Why local minima are not always the main problem.
  • Why learning rate, batch size, and noise affect generalization.
  • Why double descent breaks the classical U-shaped story.
Do not overclaim
  • Flat minima are useful intuition, not a complete theory.
  • PAC bounds are often loose for deep learning.
  • Convex intuition helps, but neural nets are not convex.
  • Double descent does not mean overfitting disappeared.

Convex vs non-convex loss

L(alpha x + (1-alpha)y) <= alpha L(x) + (1-alpha)L(y)
Convexity condition
Lloss functionObjective being minimized.
x,ytwo parameter pointsAny two points in parameter space.
alphamixing weightNumber between 0 and 1 used to interpolate between x and y.
<=bowl conditionThe function lies below the straight line between any two points.
Intuition: convex loss has no deceptive valleys; any local minimum is global.
Analogy: a perfect bowl: if you roll downhill, you eventually reach the bottom.

Linear regression with squared loss is convex. Logistic regression is convex in its weights. Deep neural networks are non-convex because layers multiply parameters together, activations create piecewise structure, and many parameter settings can represent similar functions.

Local minima vs saddle points

grad L(theta*) = 0 and H has both positive and negative eigenvalues => saddle point
What the symbols mean
grad LgradientFirst-order slope of loss.
theta*stationary pointA point where gradient is zero or nearly zero.
HHessianMatrix of second derivatives around theta*.
eigenvaluescurvature directionsPositive means bowl direction, negative means downhill escape direction.
Intuition: a saddle point is flat by first derivative, but there is still an escape direction.
Analogy: a mountain pass: flat at the center, uphill in one direction and downhill in another.

In high dimensions, saddle points are common because there are many directions where curvature can differ. Stochastic gradient noise, momentum, and curvature can help escape saddles.

Flatness, sharpness, and SAM intuition

Flat minima are regions where small parameter perturbations do not increase loss much. Sharp minima are sensitive: tiny parameter movement can hurt loss. Flatness is often linked to robustness and generalization, though exact definitions are subtle because neural network parameterization can rescale layers.

min_theta max_{||epsilon|| <= rho} L(theta + epsilon)
SAM-style objective
thetaparametersWeights being optimized.
epsilonperturbationSmall adversarial move in parameter space.
rhoradiusHow far parameters may be perturbed.
maxworst local lossLooks for the highest loss near current parameters.
minouter optimizationChooses parameters with low worst-case nearby loss.
Intuition: prefer solutions surrounded by low loss, not solutions that are good only at one exact point.
Analogy: choose a wide safe valley rather than balancing on a narrow spike.

PAC learning and sample complexity

PAC learning asks whether a model class can learn a function with high probability from finite samples. It gives language for sample complexity, hypothesis classes, and why model capacity matters.

n = O((VCdim + log(1/delta)) / epsilon)
Generalization-bound shape
nsample sizeHow many labeled examples are needed.
VCdimcapacity measureRoughly, how expressive the hypothesis class is.
deltafailure probabilityHow often the guarantee is allowed to fail.
epsilonerror toleranceHow close learned performance should be to desired performance.
Intuition: more expressive models usually need more data for uniform guarantees.
Analogy: a more flexible story can fit more evidence, but also needs more evidence to be trusted.

For deep networks, classical PAC/VC bounds are often too loose to predict practice. Still, they teach the right question: how much data is needed to trust performance beyond training examples?

Double descent

Classical bias-variance thinking predicts test error first decreases, then increases as model capacity grows. Double descent adds another regime: after the interpolation threshold, larger overparameterized models can improve again.

test error: underfit -> interpolation peak -> overparameterized descent
What the phases mean
underfittoo little capacityModel cannot represent the pattern well.
interpolation peakbarely fits train dataModel can fit training data but may be unstable and sensitive.
descentmore capacity helpsOverparameterized model can find smoother or simpler solutions in function space.
Intuition: capacity can hurt near the interpolation boundary, then help again when optimization has many good solutions.
Analogy: a tight suit is worse than both a simple loose robe and a well-tailored flexible outfit.

Double descent is one reason modern deep learning does not follow the old "bigger model always overfits more" rule. Data, optimization, regularization, and architecture still determine whether the larger model helps.

Exercises

Foundational

Classify landscapes

Explain why linear regression with MSE is convex but a two-layer neural network is not.

Show solution

MSE linear regression is quadratic in the weights. A two-layer network multiplies parameters across layers and applies nonlinear activations, creating a non-convex objective.

Intermediate

Saddle point diagnosis

If gradient is near zero but Hessian has one negative eigenvalue, what does that mean?

Show solution

It is a saddle point: locally flat by first derivative, but there exists at least one downhill direction.

Hard

SAM intuition

Explain why minimizing worst-case nearby loss might improve generalization.

Show solution

It encourages parameter regions where small perturbations do not hurt loss, which often corresponds to more robust solutions and less sensitivity to noise.

Проверь себя

Вопросы для самопроверки

  1. В чём разница между выпуклой и невыпуклой задачей оптимизации?

    В выпуклой задаче любой локальный минимум является глобальным, гарантируя сходимость градиентного спуска к оптимуму. В невыпуклых задачах (обучение нейросетей) существуют седловые точки и плохие локальные минимумы, хотя на практике нейросети редко «застревают» в них серьёзно.

  2. Почему SGD сходится медленнее, чем градиентный спуск, но используется чаще?

    SGD обновляет параметры по мини-батчу, а не по всем данным — это намного быстрее за одну итерацию и позволяет обрабатывать большие датасеты. Его шум иногда даже помогает: он действует как регуляризатор и помогает выбраться из острых локальных минимумов.

  3. Что такое learning rate schedule и зачем он нужен?

    Learning rate schedule — стратегия изменения скорости обучения η в ходе тренировки. Большой η в начале позволяет быстро найти хорошую область, малый η в конце — точно сойтись. Популярные варианты: step decay, cosine annealing, warmup + decay.

  4. Почему вторые производные (метод Ньютона) редко используются напрямую в глубоком обучении?

    Метод Ньютона требует вычисления и инвертирования матрицы Гессе размером O(p²), где p — число параметров. Для современных сетей p > 10⁸, что делает это вычислительно невозможным. Вместо этого используют квазиньютоновские методы или адаптивные оптимизаторы (Adam), которые аппроксимируют кривизну.

Как закрепить тему: Теория оптимизации

Блок связывает теорию с практикой: где применять идею, какие ошибки проверять и как развиваться дальше.

Когда применять

  • Используй знания об оптимизации, когда выбираешь или отлаживаешь optimizer.
  • При нестабильном обучении — первым делом смотри на lr, momentum и форму loss landscape.
  • Анализируй training curves совместно: скорость убывания loss + variance между прогонами.

Типичные ошибки

  • Устанавливать lr «на глаз» без lr range test или расписания.
  • Путать локальный минимум с глобальным — для большинства deep-сетей хватает любого хорошего..
  • Игнорировать curvature: Adam хорошо работает «из коробки», но fine-tuning требует понимания кривизны.

Что тюнить / проверить

  • Learning rate: основная ручка — подбирай через lr finder или grid.
  • Momentum / β₁, β₂ в Adam — редко требуют изменений, но важно при файн-тюнинге.
  • Gradient clipping и weight decay — ортогональные регуляризаторы.
