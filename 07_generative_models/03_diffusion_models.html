7.3 Diffusion Models

Diffusion learns generation as iterative denoising: corrupt real data with controlled noise, then train a model to reverse that corruption step by step.

Почему важно

Диффузионные модели вытеснили GAN как стандарт генерации изображений. Постепенное зашумление + обучение обратному процессу — простая идея, дающая фотореалистичные результаты без нестабильности GAN.

TL;DR

A diffusion model does not map random noise to an image in one jump. It learns many small denoising steps, where each step removes a predictable amount of noise from the current sample.

Forward process: destroy data in a controlled way

The forward process is fixed, not learned. At each timestep it adds a small amount of Gaussian noise. After many steps, the sample becomes almost pure noise.

q(x_t | x_{t-1}) = N(x_t; sqrt(1-beta_t) x_{t-1}, beta_t I)
Что означают символы
x_tnoisy sampleData point after t noise steps: image, audio, latent vector or embedding.
x_{t-1}previous sampleSlightly cleaner version from the previous timestep.
beta_tnoise scheduleSmall positive value controlling how much noise is added at step t.
Iidentity covarianceNoise is independent across dimensions in the basic DDPM formulation.
Интуиция: the process slowly washes structure out of the data while keeping the corruption mathematically trackable.
Аналогия: like adding fog to a photo in many tiny steps until only fog remains.
Числовой пример: if beta_t = 0.01, the step keeps about sqrt(0.99) = 0.995 of the previous signal and injects 1% noise variance.
Что это значит на практике
The schedule is a curriculum. Early timesteps teach the model to recover fine details from light corruption. Late timesteps teach it to create global structure from heavy noise.

Closed form: sample any noise level directly

DDPM has a useful closed form: instead of applying every small noising step, we can sample x_t directly from x_0 and one Gaussian noise draw.

x_t = sqrt(alpha_bar_t) x_0 + sqrt(1-alpha_bar_t) epsilon
Что означают символы
x_0clean dataOriginal training example before corruption.
alpha_bar_tremaining signalCumulative product of alpha values; decreases as t grows.
epsilonGaussian noiseRandom noise sampled from a standard normal distribution.
sqrt(...)variance scalingKeeps total variance controlled while signal is replaced by noise.
Интуиция: x_t is a weighted mixture of real signal and random noise.
Аналогия: like blending a clean photo with static using a slider.
Числовой пример: if alpha_bar_t = 0.25, then x_t keeps 0.5 of the clean signal and adds sqrt(0.75) ≈ 0.866 noise scale.

Reverse process: learn to remove noise

The reverse process is learned. A neural network receives a noisy sample and timestep, then predicts how to move one step toward cleaner data.

L_simple = E || epsilon - epsilon_theta(x_t, t) ||^2
Что означают символы
epsilontrue noiseThe exact noise that was added to create x_t during training.
epsilon_thetapredicted noiseNeural network output; theta are trainable weights.
ttimestepTells the network how noisy the input is.
||.||_2^2MSE penaltyLarge mistakes in noise prediction are penalized quadratically.
Интуиция: if the model can identify the noise, it can subtract it and recover structure.
Аналогия: like learning the hiss in an audio recording so you can remove it.
Числовой пример: if true noise is [0.3, -0.1] and prediction is [0.2, 0.0], the squared error is 0.01 + 0.01 = 0.02.

Common mistakes

  • Thinking diffusion directly predicts pixels from pure noise in one step. It predicts local denoising directions.
  • Ignoring the timestep embedding. The same noisy image means different things depending on noise level.
  • Confusing DDPM training with sampling. Training can use random timesteps; sampling must run a reverse chain.

Пошагово DDPM Sampling — from pure noise to a generated image
1 /
Шаг 01

Start from pure Gaussian noise x_T

Sample x_T ~ N(0, I). This is a tensor of the same spatial dimensions as the images you want to generate, filled entirely with independent Gaussian noise. At this point there is no structure at all — we have not started denoising yet. The sampling process will iteratively refine this over T steps.

Шаг 02

Predict noise ε with the U-Net at timestep t

Pass the current noisy sample x_t and the timestep index t into the trained neural network ε_θ(x_t, t). The network outputs a predicted noise tensor of the same shape as x_t. The timestep is embedded as a Fourier or sinusoidal feature and injected into each U-Net block so the model knows how much noise is present.

Шаг 03

Compute x_{t-1} from x_t and predicted noise

Use the DDPM reverse step formula: x_{t-1} = (1/sqrt(α_t)) * (x_t − β_t/sqrt(1−ᾱ_t) * ε_θ(x_t,t)). This subtracts the predicted noise scaled by a timestep-dependent coefficient, moving one step toward a cleaner image. The formula is derived from the posterior q(x_{t-1}|x_t, x_0).

Шаг 04

Add small noise for t > 1

For all steps except the final one (t = 1), add a small amount of Gaussian noise: x_{t-1} += sqrt(β_t) * z where z ~ N(0,I). This stochastic injection prevents the sampler from collapsing to a single deterministic point and is crucial for sample diversity. At t = 1 we skip this addition and output x_0 directly.

Шаг 05

Output x_0 — the generated image

After running Steps 2–4 for t = T, T−1, …, 1, the final x_0 is the generated image. Each step removed a small portion of the original noise, guided by the neural network's prediction. The quality depends on the number of steps, the noise schedule, and whether guidance (CFG) was applied during the reverse process.

Проверь себя

Diffusion models — schedule, sampling, and objectives

  1. Why did the original DDPM paper use 1000 diffusion steps?

    1000 steps makes each individual forward-process step add only a very small amount of noise (β_t ≈ 0.0001 to 0.02). This ensures the reverse step can be well-approximated by a Gaussian — the theoretical justification for the DDPM objective. Fewer steps would make each step a large non-Gaussian jump, violating the assumption that the reverse posterior is also approximately Gaussian.

  2. What does DDIM trade off compared to DDPM sampling?

    DDIM (Denoising Diffusion Implicit Models) replaces the stochastic Markov reverse process with a deterministic one. This allows skipping timesteps — sampling with 20–50 steps instead of 1000 without retraining the model. The trade-off: slightly lower diversity in the outputs because the stochastic noise injection is removed, but much faster generation with comparable quality.

  3. Why does DDPM predict the noise ε rather than x_0 directly?

    Predicting ε is an equivalent re-parameterization of predicting x_0, but empirically more stable to train. The noise ε is always drawn from N(0,I) regardless of the input, giving the prediction target a consistent scale and distribution across all timesteps. Predicting x_0 directly at high noise levels forces the network to reconstruct complex structure from almost pure noise, which is harder to optimize.

Что ломается без этого

If the noise schedule beta values are set too large (e.g. jumping to β = 0.5 in just 100 steps instead of 1000), the forward process destroys the signal too quickly. The model sees training samples where x_100 is already indistinguishable from pure noise, so it cannot learn a meaningful reverse direction. At inference time the denoiser predicts near-zero noise at every step, and the chain never moves from pure noise toward a real image — generation produces random blurry patches.

Проверь себя

Вопросы для самопроверки

  1. Как работает forward process в диффузионных моделях?

    Forward process постепенно добавляет Гауссов шум к данным x₀: q(xₜ|xₜ₋₁) = N(xₜ; √(1-βₜ)xₜ₋₁, βₜI). После T шагов (обычно T=1000) данные становятся чистым шумом xT ~ N(0,I). Аналитически: q(xₜ|x₀) = N(xₜ; √ᾱₜx₀, (1-ᾱₜ)I), что позволяет сэмплировать любой шаг напрямую.

  2. Чему учится нейросеть в диффузионной модели?

    Нейросеть ε_θ(xₜ, t) учится предсказывать добавленный шум ε при данном зашумлённом xₜ и шаге t. Loss = MSE(ε_θ(xₜ,t), ε). При генерации итеративно «убираем» предсказанный шум из xₜ, получая xₜ₋₁, и повторяем T раз.

  3. Что такое classifier-free guidance и как он улучшает генерацию?

    CFG обучает одну модель с условием c и без (с вероятностью 10-20% заменяем c на null). При генерации: ε_guided = ε_uncond + w·(ε_cond - ε_uncond). Коэффициент w (guidance scale) усиливает влияние условия. Высокий w → более качественные, но менее разнообразные изображения.

  4. В чём основной недостаток диффузионных моделей по сравнению с GAN?

    Диффузионные модели требуют T=50-1000 шагов денойзинга при генерации — намного медленнее GAN (один forward pass). DDIM и другие ускоренные семплеры снижают до 10-50 шагов без потери качества. Consistency models пытаются дойти до 1-2 шагов.

Как закрепить тему: Диффузионные модели

Блок связывает теорию с практикой: где применять идею, какие ошибки проверять и как развиваться дальше.

Когда применять

  • Используй DDIM для ускоренного sampling (10-50 шагов вместо 1000).
  • Guidance scale (CFG) 7-12 — хороший диапазон для баланса качества и разнообразия.
  • Fine-tune через DreamBooth или LoRA, не с нуля — требует минимум данных.

Типичные ошибки

  • Использовать слишком высокий CFG scale (>15) — изображения пересвечиваются.
  • Не нормализовать входные изображения перед обучением — нужна нормализация [-1, 1].
  • Игнорировать noise schedule: linear vs cosine по-разному влияет на качество.

Что тюнить / проверить

  • Number of diffusion steps (inference) — скорость vs качество.
  • Guidance scale — сила условия: выше → точнее, но менее разнообразно.
  • Noise schedule: cosine обычно лучше linear для изображений высокого разрешения.
