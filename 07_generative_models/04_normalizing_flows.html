7.4 Normalizing Flows

A normalizing flow turns a simple distribution into a complex one through a chain of invertible transformations with exact likelihood.

Почему важно

Normalizing flows — единственный класс генеративных моделей с точным likelihood. Invertible transformations позволяют точно вычислить p(x) через change of variables.

Why flows exist

VAEs give tractable training but only approximate likelihood. GANs generate sharp samples but do not give likelihood. Diffusion gives high quality but sampling is iterative. Flows occupy a different point: exact likelihood and exact sampling through an invertible map.

The price is architectural: every transformation must be invertible and must have a cheap Jacobian determinant.

Change of variables

Start with a simple latent variable z, usually standard Gaussian. Apply an invertible function x = f(z). The density of x is obtained by correcting the density of z by the local volume change.

p_X(x) = p_Z(f^{-1}(x)) | det d f^{-1}(x) / d x |
Что означают символы
p_X(x)data densityProbability density assigned to the observed sample x.
p_Z(z)base densitySimple distribution, often N(0,I), where likelihood is easy.
f^{-1}inverse mapMaps data back into latent space.
det Jvolume correctionMeasures how much the transformation expands or compresses space around x.
Интуиция: probability mass is conserved, but density changes when space is stretched or squeezed.
Аналогия: the same amount of paint looks thinner when spread over a larger area.
Числовой пример: if a 1D transform doubles length, local density halves because the same mass occupies twice the interval.

Log-likelihood form

In practice we optimize log-likelihood. Products become sums, and a chain of K invertible transforms adds K log-determinant corrections.

log p_X(x) = log p_Z(z) + sum_k log |det J_k|
Что означают символы
Kflow depthNumber of invertible transformations in the chain.
J_kJacobianMatrix of local derivatives for transform k.
log |det J_k|log volume changeHow transform k changes density locally.
zlatent codeThe base-space representation of x after applying inverse transforms.
Интуиция: likelihood is base Gaussian plausibility plus all geometric corrections.
Аналогия: like tracking currency exchange through several banks, adding each fee adjustment.
Числовой пример: if log p_Z(z)=-2.0 and two transforms add 0.4 and -0.1, then log p_X(x)=-1.7.

RealNVP coupling layer

A fully general invertible neural network has an expensive determinant. RealNVP solves this by splitting the vector: one part stays fixed, while the other part is scaled and shifted based on the fixed part.

y_a = x_a, y_b = x_b * exp(s(x_a)) + t(x_a)
Что означают символы
x_a, x_bsplit inputTwo parts of the vector or image channels.
s(x_a)scale networkPredicts how much to stretch x_b based on x_a.
t(x_a)shift networkPredicts how much to move x_b based on x_a.
odotelementwise productEach dimension is scaled independently.
Интуиция: keep one half stable so the other half can be transformed with an easy inverse.
Аналогия: holding one side of a sheet fixed while stretching and moving the other side.
Числовой пример: if x_b=2, s=log(1.5), t=-1, then y_b=2*1.5-1=2.

Пошагово Change of Variables — how a flow computes exact likelihood
1 /
Шаг 01

Start with a simple prior z ~ N(0, I)

Define a base distribution p_Z — typically a standard multivariate Gaussian. This distribution is trivial to evaluate and sample from. The goal is to transform it into a complex data distribution p_X while keeping the ability to compute exact log-probabilities. Every flow starts from this tractable base.

Шаг 02

Apply an invertible transformation f: z → x

Pass z through a learned invertible function x = f(z). Because f is invertible, we can also compute z = f⁻¹(x) for any observed data point x. The transformation can be a single coupling layer or a chain of K layers: x = f_K ∘ f_{K−1} ∘ … ∘ f_1(z). Each layer must be both invertible and differentiable.

Шаг 03

Compute log-likelihood via log |det J|

The change-of-variables formula gives: log p_X(x) = log p_Z(z) + log |det J_{f⁻¹}(x)|. The Jacobian determinant corrects for how much f stretches or compresses local volume. For a chain of K transforms: log p_X(x) = log p_Z(z) + Σ_k log |det J_k|. Coupling layers make each J_k triangular, so its determinant is just the product of diagonal entries — computable in O(d) time.

Шаг 04

Exact likelihood enables direct training

Because we can compute log p_X(x) exactly for any training example, we maximize the log-likelihood directly using gradient descent — no ELBO, no adversarial game. This also means flows can be used for density estimation (computing p(x) for anomaly detection), lossless data compression, and as flexible approximate posteriors in variational inference.

Проверь себя

Normalizing flows — invertibility and exact likelihood

  1. Why must the transformations in a normalizing flow be invertible?

    Invertibility is required for two reasons. First, to compute log p_X(x) during training you need z = f⁻¹(x) — you must be able to map data back to the base space. Second, the change-of-variables formula is only valid for bijective (one-to-one) maps: if f loses information, two different x values could map to the same z, making the probability calculation undefined. Invertibility guarantees a one-to-one correspondence between x-space and z-space.

  2. What makes computing det(J) tractable in coupling layers?

    In a general invertible network, computing det(J) costs O(d³) because d×d Jacobian matrix determinant requires full decomposition. Coupling layers split the input into two parts: one part passes through unchanged, and the other is scaled and shifted based on the first. The resulting Jacobian is triangular (lower or upper). The determinant of a triangular matrix is just the product of its diagonal entries, computable in O(d) — making flows practical at high dimensions.

  3. Why do flows give exact likelihood while VAEs only give a lower bound?

    VAEs introduce an approximate posterior q_φ(z|x) because the true posterior p(z|x) is intractable. The gap between ELBO and the true log p(x) is the KL divergence between approximate and true posterior, which is always non-negative but unknown. Flows avoid this entirely: because f is a bijection, z = f⁻¹(x) is unique and deterministic, so log p_X(x) = log p_Z(f⁻¹(x)) + log |det J| is computed exactly with no approximation.

Что ломается без этого

If a flow transformation is not square — meaning the output dimension differs from the input dimension — the Jacobian matrix is not square and its determinant is undefined. The entire change-of-variables formula breaks down: you cannot compute log p_X(x) because the volume correction term has no meaning for a non-square linear map. Unlike VAEs which can project to a lower-dimensional latent, flows must maintain constant dimensionality at every layer.

Вопросы для самопроверки

  1. Что такое normalizing flow и что делает его «нормализующим»?

    Normalizing flow — цепочка обратимых дифференцируемых преобразований, отображающих простое распределение (N(0,I)) в сложное p(x). «Нормализующий» означает, что вероятность «нормализуется»: по формуле change-of-variables p(x) = p(z)|det(J)|⁻¹, вычисляемой точно.

  2. Почему flows вычисляют точное правдоподобие, тогда как VAE и GAN — нет?

    Flow — биективное отображение, поэтому log p(x) = log p(z) + log|det J| вычисляется точно. VAE оптимизирует ELBO (нижнюю оценку). GAN не имеет явного правдоподобия вовсе. Точное правдоподобие важно для сжатия данных (lossless compression) и аномалии-детекции.

  3. Что такое coupling layers и почему они нужны в Real-NVP?

    Coupling layers разбивают вектор на две части: первая проходит без изменений, вторая трансформируется как функция первой. Якобиан треугольный → det вычисляется за O(d) вместо O(d³). Это делает flows практичными: обратимость + дешёвый det.

  4. Какие задачи решают normalizing flows, кроме генерации?

    Flows используются для: (1) точной оценки плотности (outlier detection), (2) variational inference как более гибкий posterior, (3) нейронные ODEs (continuous normalizing flows), (4) сжатия данных (bijective → lossless). В молекулярном моделировании (AlphaFold) — для генерации структур белков.

Блок связывает теорию с практикой: где применять идею, какие ошибки проверять и как развиваться дальше.

Когда применять

  • Используй flows для задач с точной оценкой плотности (anomaly detection, lossless compression).
  • Glow и RealNVP — хорошие базовые архитектуры для старта.
  • Точное log-likelihood — главное преимущество над GAN/VAE для диагностики.

Типичные ошибки

  • Не проверять invertibility численно — ошибки в coupling layers могут нарушить биективность.
  • Игнорировать вычислительную стоимость якобиана при масштабировании.
  • Путать flows с VAE: flows точные, VAE дают нижнюю оценку.

Что тюнить / проверить

  • Число coupling layers — глубина модели: больше = выразительнее, дороже.
  • Ширина сетей внутри coupling layers.
  • Тип coupling: additive vs affine — affine мощнее, но менее стабилен.
